FORM OF TERMS AND CONDITIONS OF SERVICE

Sotho Minigrid Portfolio SPV (PTY) LTD (the "Mini-Grid Provider") has developed this standard customer contract (this "Agreement") to set forth the service that we commit to give to you as well as the conditions to which we expect you to adhere.

The words "we," "us," and "our" refer to the Mini-Grid Provider, and the words "you" and "your" refer to the customer.

  • 1. APPLICATION OF GENERAL CONDITIONS

    These are our standard conditions with you. By entering into this Agreement, you agree that you are a customer and are bound by the terms and conditions set out herein.

  • 2. SALE AND SUPPLY OF ELECTRICITY

    • 2.1 This Agreement sets out the standard terms and conditions under which the Mini-Grid Provider supplies 60 amps capacity (minimum) of single phase 220VAC 50Hz electricity to you (the "Service"). The Service is carried out by means of connection between the Mini-Grid Provider's distribution network and the single main service panel load center (or ready board) including earth leakage and circuit protections (the "Load Center") with a unique Account ID to be provided upon acceptance of this agreement.

    • 2.2 All customers within the area serviced by the Mini-Grid Provider (the "Project Area") have equal access to electric service on these terms.

    • 2.3 Customers with 3-phase, voltage levels above 220VAC or other special electricity service needs can contact us in order to negotiate specific terms and conditions of the Services.

    • 2.4 This Agreement works in conjunction with the standard customer electricity connection and distribution technical requirements stipulated in the Lesotho Grid Code or other relevant standards issued by the Lesotho Electricity and Water Authority.

    • 2.5 If you have electricity supplied by us to multiple premises at different sites in the Project Area or in the same building, this contract applies separately and independently to each of those premises (or accounts).

  • 3. CUSTOMER DETAILS FORM AND INFORMATION SHARING AND PRIVACY

    • 3.1 Customers entering into this Agreement with the Mini-Grid Provider shall complete the Customers Supply Details Form (Schedule A to this Agreement).

    • 3.2 You must provide us with your contact details (e.g., a mobile number) and we are allowed to use these details to contact you in relation to your account and this Agreement.

      • 3.2.1 By signing this Agreement you consent to receive text message notifications. Further options of notifications can be selected in the Customers Supply Details Form.

    • 3.3 All information that you give us must be truthful, correct and accurate. You must tell us about any change in the information as soon as reasonably possible. It is important that you give us the information that we need promptly so that there is no delay while we proceed.

    • 3.4 You shall promptly and accurately notify us of any changes to the following:

      • 3.4.1 mobile phone number(s);

      • 3.4.2 access to meter; or

      • 3.4.3 household wiring and electrical equipment that may be dangerous and or reducing quality of electricity supply.

    • 3.5 We commit to protect the privacy of the information you provide, in accordance with law. Your information will not be shared except in ways relating to electricity use for the purpose of statistical study of consumption patterns.

  • 4. PROVISION OF SUPPLY

    • 4.1 Before we can provide you with the Service, you need to:

      • 4.1.1 make an application for the Service by reading, understanding and completing the requirements of this Agreement;

      • 4.1.2 provide complete details about yourself and your premises for the reasons that will have been explained;

      • 4.1.3 demonstrate that your premises are accessible or within reaching distance of our operations; and

      • 4.1.4 pay any required charges as set out in Schedule B.

    • 4.2 Our connection to your premises and your ability to receive the Service from us will be subject to us getting any necessary consent from you and your neighboring occupiers along the power lines near your premises. Where necessary, in order for you to receive Service, you shall obtain such consent from neighboring occupiers.

  • 5. ACCESS AND METER LOCATION AND PREMISES

    • 5.1 We will install a meter close to the premises; or we may (at an additional cost) assist in installing a digital read out panel inside the premises. Only authorized persons will service the meter.

    • 5.2 The customer shall ensure the meter is kept in good condition. If you notice damage of any kind to the meter, you must inform us as soon as reasonably possible.

    • 5.3 You shall allow our employees, contractors and agents access to the premises:

      • 5.3.1 to read, install, inspect, remove or replace meters where necessary;

      • 5.3.2 to inspect, repair or replace other equipment; and

      • 5.3.3 where we are required to comply with law.

    • 5.4 In addition, we may place our electric equipment (poles and anchors) in the yard of the premises. If we ask you to accept our equipment, we will take reasonable steps to get equipment by a route that will minimise any disruption.

    • 5.5 Near the metering equipment agricultural activities may continue but, for safety reasons, trees and structures should not be within 2 meters of our equipment. We shall be able to reach the metering point at all times.

    • 5.6 After installation of the metering equipment, if structures or trees are found to be obstructing the 2 meter space around the meter, you will be responsible for the costs of removing either the meter or the obstruction. For avoidance of doubt, metering equipment includes the cables connected to it.

  • 6. WIRING OF YOUR PREMISES

    • 6.1 Internal wiring of the premises is the responsibility of the customer and shall be carried out in accordance with relevant electrical wiring regulations. At your request we may provide a recommendation of electrical installers for the internal wiring of your premises. We take no responsibility for the work or materials of a third party installer.

    • 6.2 We will not connect you to the Service unless there is a Load Center at the premises.

    • 6.3 Customers may purchase the Load Center from us or from a third party. If the Load Center is purchased from a third party, we will inspect it to confirm that it meets the required standard before connection. If so, the Load Center purchased from a third party that does not pass the inspection will be at the customer's cost.

    • 6.4 We will own all equipment between the connection and the Load Center, including the meter.

  • 7. LINE EXTENSIONS

    • 7.1 Where construction is required before the Service, a line extension will be included and you can contact us directly to arrange the Service. We may require consent for an extension of the premises, and this means the customer will be responsible for the construction costs of which they will receive an estimate before the work begins.

    • 7.2 By “Construction” is meant any work connected with the construction, extension, installation, repair, alteration, improvement, conversion, removal or demolition of our equipment, including temporary works.

  • 8. CUSTOMER SERVICES

    • 8.1 Account/meter deposit

      • To open an account, you must pay a connection fee of 500 LSL per meter connection. If a building has multiple units requiring connection, each unit will have a separate connection fee.

    • 8.2 Units sold/metering

      • The amount of electricity sold will be measured in kilowatt hours ("kWhs").

    • 8.3 Method of paying for electricity (Pre-paid system)

      • 8.3.1 You will pay for the Service using mobile money (e.g. M-PESA). Your purchase will be credited according to our tariffs as described in Section 8.6 below.

      • 8.3.2 Your electricity consumption costs will be deducted from your prepaid balance.

      • 8.3.3 Accounts that reach a balance of 0.00 LSL will be suspended. If your balance reaches 0 LSL or below, you will receive a notification from us by message if the network is working properly.

      • 8.3.4 It is your responsibility as a customer to regularly check your account balance.

      • 8.3.5 You will not receive a paper statement from us. Balance information is available via message or mobile phone.

    • 8.4 Reconnection and reconnection surcharge

      • 8.4.1 When the Service is not available due to reasons other than non-payment, we will take reasonable steps to restore the Service within one day of identifying the cause of the problem.

      • 8.4.2 When disconnection occurs under Section 8.3.3 above, the Service will be restored once the outstanding balance is paid or a minimum credit of 5 LSL is applied.

      • 8.4.3 In the case of disconnection under Section 8.4.3 above, the Service will be restored upon payment of the outstanding balance.

      • 8.4.4 You will be required to pay the reconnection costs under Section 8.3.3 or Section 8.4.3 above to have the Service restored. Such costs may include the cost of visiting your premises to assist with reconnection.

    • 8.5 Tariffs

      • 8.5.1 You agree to our tariffs as set out in the applicable tariff schedule. This tariff is governed by an annual escalation or decrease mechanism in accordance with the consumer price index as calculated by the Lesotho Bureau of Statistics. You may obtain details of our tariffs at any time during 12 months added in Schedule C (Tariffs and Charges) and on our website 1pwrafrica.com or by calling our Customer Service Department on the number in Section 16.1 below.

      • 8.5.2 We will include tax as appropriate in our tariffs where required.

      • 8.5.3 We may change our prices (including by making any discount to the unit discount rate applicable), or make a change to any of these terms. If we increase our prices or make a change to any of these terms that disadvantages you, we will notify you of the changes in an appropriate format as indicated in Section 10.5.

  • 9. SERVITUDE

    • 9.1 For the purpose of providing the Service, you grant us a servitude over your premises where we will install the necessary cables, meters and other equipment. The customer agrees to cooperate with us in accessing this equipment.

    • 9.2 The servitude includes the land below and the airspace above for any purpose related to the provision of the Service to you or to those in close proximity to you. It also includes such portions of the common property and land adjacent to your property as may be necessary for the provision of the Service to you or to those in close proximity to you.

    • 9.3 The customer agrees that all meters, cables and other equipment installed at your premises (other than the Load Center) shall remain the property of the Mini-Grid Provider.

    • 9.4 When you need to relocate meters, cables and other equipment, you will be responsible for the costs of relocation and you will indicate where they should be reinstalled (which must be an agreed location).

    • 9.5 This Section 9 shall not be affected by the termination of this Agreement.

    • 10.1 We may terminate this Agreement if:

      • 10.1.1 You do not have the legal right to enter into this Agreement;

      • 10.1.2 Your connection or use of electricity interferes with the distribution network or disturbs other customers;

      • 10.1.3 You transfer supply to a person whom we consider to be a third party customer;

      • 10.1.4 You tamper with our meters;

      • 10.1.5 You fail to meet all of your obligations under this Agreement;

      • 10.1.6 You use electricity without permission or steal electricity;

      • 10.1.7 We are required to do so by applicable law or court order; or

      • 10.1.8 It is no longer economically viable for us to supply you electricity for reasons beyond our control.

    • 10.2 You may terminate this Agreement and the Mini-Grid Provider will refund any required balance if applicable, if:

      • 10.2.1 We lose our right under applicable law or regulation to provide the Service;

      • 10.2.2 You no longer wish to receive the Service from us; or

      • 10.2.3 You have no outstanding obligations to us under this Agreement.

    • 10.3 Subject to the terms herein, either you or we may terminate this Agreement by giving 30 days written notice to the non-terminating party.

    • 10.4 Termination of this Agreement will not relieve either party of any liability or obligation accrued prior to the date of termination or deprive the non-defaulting party of any remedy otherwise available.

Notwithstanding the above we reserve the right to disconnect service at our sole discretion pending the resolution of any dispute referred to the Authority.
